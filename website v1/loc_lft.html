How we use location-related information


We use location-related information that you allow us to receive if you turn on the Location device setting. This includes things like your GPS location and, depending on the operating system you're using, other device signals.

We also receive and use some location-related information even if Location is turned off. This includes:
  1. IP addresses, which we use to estimate your general location. We can use IP addresses to estimate your specific location if it’s necessary to protect the safety and security of you or others.
  2. Your and others' activity on our platform, like check-ins and events
  3. Information you give us directly, like if you enter your current city on your profile, or provide your address

We use location-related information, such as your current location, where you live, the places you like to go and the businesses and people you're near, to do the things like:
  1. Provide, personalize and improve our platform, including ads, for you and others. See an example.
  2. suspicious activity and help keep your account secure.

How to review and manage your location information:

  1. Navigate to Settings.
  2. Expand “Location data” to review and change your location settings.
